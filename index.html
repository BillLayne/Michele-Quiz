🧮 Coinsurance Calculator & Learning Tool

📚 What is Coinsurance? (In Simple Terms)

Think of coinsurance as an "honesty check" that makes sure you're insuring your property for its real value.

Here's the deal:

  • Most policies require you to insure your property for AT LEAST 80% of what it would cost to rebuild/replace
  • If you under-insure (carry less than 80%), you get PENALIZED at claim time
  • The penalty means insurance won't pay the full claim - YOU pay the difference!

Why do insurance companies care?
Because if you only insure a $500,000 building for $200,000 and pay lower premiums, that's not fair. The coinsurance clause makes sure everyone pays their fair share.

📐 Coinsurance Penalty Formula (Industry Standard)

Step 1: Amount Required = Property Value × Coinsurance %
Step 2: Compare Amount Carried to Amount Required
Step 3: If under-insured:
    Insurance Pays = (Amount Carried ÷ Amount Required) × Loss Amount
Step 4: Subtract Deductible from payment
Step 5: Penalty = What you lose due to under-insurance

💰 ACV (Actual Cash Value) Calculator

📚 What is ACV? (Actual Cash Value)

ACV = What something is worth TODAY after years of use

Simple formula:

ACV = Replacement Cost - Depreciation

Example: Your TV cost $2,000 new. Today, a similar new TV costs $1,500 (replacement cost). Your TV is 3 years old and worth 40% less (depreciation). ACV = $1,500 - ($1,500 × 40%) = $1,500 - $600 = $900

Why it matters: If your policy pays ACV (not Replacement Cost), you get the depreciated value. You'd receive $900 for a TV that costs $1,500 to replace - you pay $600 out-of-pocket!

Replacement Cost coverage is better - it pays the full cost to replace with new (minus deductible), but costs more in premiums.

⚡ Quick Reference Guide

Essential formulas, percentages, and NC-specific requirements at your fingertips!

📐 ESSENTIAL FORMULAS

🧮 Coinsurance Penalty Formula

Insurance Pays = (Amount Carried / Amount Required) × Loss
Then subtract deductible

Amount Required = Property Value × Coinsurance %
Example: $500K value × 80% = $400K required

💰 Actual Cash Value (ACV) Formula

ACV = Replacement Cost - Depreciation
OR
ACV = Replacement Cost × (100% - Depreciation %)

Example: $1,500 replacement - 40% depreciation
= $1,500 - $600 = $900 ACV

🏢 Business Income Formula

Business Income = (Net Profit + Continuing Expenses) × Time Period

Example: ($30K profit + $20K expenses) × 3 months
= $50K/month × 3 = $150K

🏠 HOMEOWNERS COVERAGE PERCENTAGES

Coverage A (Dwelling) = Base amount (e.g., $300,000)
Coverage B (Other Structures) = 10% of A = $30,000
Coverage C (Personal Property) = 50% of A = $150,000
Coverage D (Loss of Use) = 20% of A = $60,000
Coverage E (Liability) = $100,000 minimum (standard)
Coverage F (Medical Payments) = $1,000-$5,000

Remember: B=10%, C=50%, D=20%

🏛️ NC-SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS

🚗 NC Auto Minimum Liability (Until July 1, 2025)

30/60/25:
• $30,000 per person injured
• $60,000 per accident (total injuries)
• $25,000 property damage

⚠️ CHANGES July 1, 2025 to 50/100/50!
$50K per person / $100K per accident / $50K property

⏰ NC Important Timelines

• 10 days - Acknowledge claim receipt
• 15 days - Notice for mid-term cancellation
• 45 days - Notice for non-renewal
• 30 days - Rate filing review period
• 3 years - Record retention requirement
• 3 years - SDIP points stay on record

👨‍💼 NC Producer License Requirements

• Age: 18 years minimum
• Pre-licensing: 20 hours Property + 20 hours Casualty
• Exam: 150 questions, 2.5 hours, 70% to pass
• Continuing Education: 24 hours every 2 years (includes 3 hours ethics)
• Workers' Comp Required: 3+ employees
• Controlled Business Limit: Max 50% of total premiums

⚠️ COMMON HO-3 SUB-LIMITS (Watch Out!)

💍 Jewelry, watches, furs: $1,500 total (theft only)
💵 Money, coins, precious metals: $200 total
💳 Securities, deeds, tickets: $1,500 total
📺 Electronics: $2,500 total (some policies)
🔫 Firearms: $2,500 total
🚤 Watercraft, trailers: $1,500 total
🏆 Business property: $2,500 on premises, $500 off

💡 Solution: Schedule valuable items separately (no sub-limits, no deductible, broader coverage!)

🚗 AUTO LIABILITY LIMITS EXPLAINED

Example: 50/100/50 means:

$50,000 = Maximum per PERSON injured
$100,000 = Maximum per ACCIDENT (all people combined)
$50,000 = Maximum property damage

Example Scenario: You injure 3 people
• Person A: $80,000 injuries → Insurance pays $50,000, you owe $30,000!
• Person B: $55,000 injuries → Insurance pays $50,000, you owe $5,000!
• Person C: $40,000 injuries → Insurance pays $0! (Already paid $100K max)

You personally owe: $75,000! 😱
